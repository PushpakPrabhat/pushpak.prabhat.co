Blender is incredible — and free! But it has a steep learning curve. After 3+ years of daily use, here are the five tips that made the biggest difference in my work.

## 1. Master Geometry Nodes

Geometry Nodes aren't just for procedural modeling. I use them for:
- Scatter systems for environments
- Parametric motion graphics
- Quick prototyping of complex shapes

The time investment to learn them pays off tenfold.

## 2. Use HDRI Maps for Lighting

Stop using point lights for everything. A good HDRI map from Poly Haven (they're free!) will instantly make your renders look 10x more realistic.

## 3. Learn the Shader Editor

Custom shaders are what separate amateur renders from professional ones. Spend time understanding:
- Principled BSDF parameters
- Node-based material creation
- Procedural textures

## 4. Optimize Your Viewport

Working in a laggy viewport kills creativity. My settings:
- Use EEVEE for viewport preview
- Switch to Cycles only for final render
- Use collection visibility to hide complex objects when not needed

## 5. Keyboard Shortcuts Are Everything

Learn these by heart:
- `Shift+A` — Add menu
- `Ctrl+R` — Loop cut
- `Alt+Click` — Select loop
- `Ctrl+B` — Bevel
- `/` — Local view (isolate selected)

These five changes took my Blender workflow from frustrating to flowing. What are your favorite Blender tips? Share below! 👇
